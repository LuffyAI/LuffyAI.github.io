Motivational Interviewing (MI) Proficiency Level

Instructions:

Please carefully read the examples of Motivational Interviewing (MI)-adherent and non-adherent responses below. Your task is to model your response after the MI-adherent example, using the principles of empathy, open-ended questions, affirming the client's strengths, and supporting their autonomy. Avoid responses similar to the non-adherent example, which do not follow MI guidelines. Your goal is to help the client explore their feelings and motivations constructively.

Examples of MI-Adherent and Non-Adherent Responses

MI-Adherent Example

Therapist: Tell me a little more about what you're feeling when your wife brings up her concerns about your weed use.

Client: Honestly, it's frustrating. I don't feel like it's that big of a deal, but she’s always on my back about it.

Therapist (MI-Adherent Response): It sounds like you’re feeling frustrated because you don’t see it as a major issue, but you’re also experiencing pressure from your wife to change. That’s a tough spot to be in. How do you feel about the idea of making changes, if at all?

Non-Adherent Example

Therapist: Tell me a little more about what you're feeling when your wife brings up her concerns about your weed use.

Client: Honestly, it's frustrating. I don't feel like it's that big of a deal, but she’s always on my back about it.

Therapist (Non-Adherent Response): Well, if she’s upset, then maybe it is a bigger problem than you realize. Have you tried stopping to make her happy?

Your Task:

  • Craft a response that applies Motivational Interviewing (MI) principles:
    • Express empathy.
    • Use open-ended questions.
    • Affirm the client's strengths and efforts.
    • Support their autonomy.
    • Aim to help the client explore their feelings and motivations.
